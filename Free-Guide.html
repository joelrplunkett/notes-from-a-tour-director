Free Europe prep checklist

Europe Travel Checklist

The simple stuff that makes a big difference once you’re actually on the road.

Written from a Tour Director’s point of view: practical, realistic, and focused on the small things travellers often overlook before leaving home.

✈️ Before You Leave

Passport — check expiry

You want at least 6 months left… the amount of tours I’ve started with people missing for this exact reason.

They couldn’t leave the country. Airlines won’t let you fly.

Have hard copies and soft copies saved securely in the cloud.

Where are you actually keeping it? Travel days? Walking around cities? Do you need it on you? Vatican? ID checks? Police?

Can you trust the hotel safe? Do you carry it?

👉 Don’t leave this as a “figure it out later” thing.

Bank / cards — make sure they’ll work overseas

Know your PIN properly, off by heart.

I’ve seen people pull out brand new travel cards… PIN written on a piece of paper in the wallet.

Insurance won’t cover that. They’ll call it negligence.

If it’s a new card, try it at home first. Know what number you’re calling from abroad if it doesn’t work.

👉 When things go wrong, you’re rarely calm and composed.

Travel insurance — have a rough idea what it covers

Flight delay? Missed connection? Lost luggage?

If you bought it through a tour operator, they usually can’t confirm details.

They’re not the insurer. Totally normal.

👉 Worth opening the policy and skimming the key bits.

Copies of documents — passport, insurance, tickets

I scan them, print them, fold them, and keep them in a zip-lock in my suitcase.

Phone copy + physical backup = covered.

Arrival plan — how are you getting from the airport to your hotel?

Got Uber? Check it now.

Look at time + price at the same time of day you’re arriving. Traffic matters.

Booked a transfer?

👉 You’re not getting ripped off — you’re paying for reliability.

Have all of this sorted early.
Not in the last few days when everything speeds up.

After a long flight, somewhere unfamiliar… simple things suddenly aren’t simple.

🧳 Packing — the realistic version

Layers > outfits

Weather moves. Don’t pack like it won’t.

Check a 10–14 day forecast. Webcams help more than you’d think.

Comfortable shoes — already worn in

Not brand new. Ever.

Cobblestones, uneven streets, walking everywhere…

👉 I’ve seen people hit 10–20k steps a day without planning to.

Start walking now.

  • proper band-aids — fabric ones
  • expect stairs — bathrooms are often downstairs
  • showers are often bath/shower combos

Plug adapter

UK ≠ Europe

Switzerland ≠ Italy

👉 Want to charge your phone from bed? Good luck if you haven’t thought this through.

Medication

  • Bring what you need + a little extra.
  • Keep it in your carry-on.

You’re out for the day… medication back in the hotel?

👉 No good.

On a coach tour, if it’s under the coach in your suitcase?

👉 You’re not getting it easily.

Luggage

  • You’ll move it more than you think.
  • Hotel rooms aren’t built for big suitcases.

Big bed = less space. That’s often the trade.

Travelling with a partner?

👉 It rarely stays “your suitcase / my suitcase”.

Things move. Plan for it.

Pack so you can actually manage it yourself.

Lifting it onto a bed? Moving it in a tight room?

👉 If it’s awkward at home, it’s worse when you’re tired.

Tour Director note: Every year I see people bring too much… and deal with it every single day after that.

🛬 Arrival Day

  • Don’t plan anything major.
  • Expect to feel tired or slightly off.
  • Know your hotel name and address.
  • Give yourself time to settle in.

Joining a tour?

Arrive a day or two early if you can.

  • Day 1: arrive + recover.
  • Day 2: light exploring.
  • Day 3: start properly.

Tour Director note: The first day quietly sets the tone… most people realise that after they’ve pushed it too far.

💳 Money

  • Card that works internationally.
  • Small amount of local cash.
  • Contactless works almost everywhere.

…but not everywhere.

Laundry, tips, smaller places — still cash.

👉 Running out of cash at the wrong moment happens more than you’d expect.

🧠 The Stuff People Don’t Think About

Jet lag is worse than expected.

Overpacking becomes a daily frustration.

👉 Not once… every day.

Free time feels shorter than you think.

People plan what they want to do, not how long things actually take.

Small logistics matter.

Bags, timing, directions…

👉 This is what quietly shapes the whole trip.

You can’t see everything. People live in these cities their whole lives and still haven’t seen it all.

Sit down. Have a coffee. Watch the world go by. That’s part of it.

✔️ Quick Final Check

Before you leave:

  • I know how I’m getting to my first hotel.
  • I’ve packed for comfort, not just photos.
  • I’ve kept day one simple.

If you’ve got that, you’re already ahead of most travellers.
